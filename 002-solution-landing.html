As a renewable energy source, the sun’s power is a readily available solution to your long-term energy needs and our collective goal of creating sustainable communities. In addition to reducing our reliance on fossil fuels, solar energy offers homeowners a wide array of benefits.

Energy independence

Generate your own power right from your rooftop or property to reduce your exposure to rising electricity prices and the frustrating uncertainty of power outages.

Utility cost savings

Significantly reduce your monthly electricity bills and even earn credit for the excess power you send back to the grid through net metering!

Sustainability

Lower your carbon footprint and take a tangible step toward a net-zero future by powering your home with renewable energy.

Maintaining Curb Appeal on a Waterfront Home

We recently installed 33 solar panels and a connected EV charger on a waterfront home in southwestern Ontario. The 12 kW system was specifically designed to capture the sun’s rays on just the East and West elevations to minimize visibility of the panels on both the front (roadside) and back (waterfront) sides of the home – in turn, increasing the value of the home without compromising curb appeal. This new solar energy system will provide 100% of the homeowners’ power needs while reducing both their hydro bills and carbon footprint as they move closer to retirement.

Homes across the country currently have access to up to a $5,000 grant for energy efficiency retrofits through the Canada Greener Homes Grant program. Available until March 2024. Find out if your home is eligible.

Up to $40,000 interest-free loan

Natural Resources Canada is currently offering homeowners across the country up to a $40,000 interest-free loan for major energy efficiency retrofits through the Canada Greener Homes Loan program. Find our if your home is eligible.

Is my roof strong enough to support a solar energy system?

Before installing a solar energy system on your rooftop, an engineering review and roof inspection will be conducted to determine whether your rooftop is strong enough to support the added load. Engineering approval takes place before we apply for your (mandatory) building permit.
